# Abbreviations, Numbers, and Symbols

This is a working list of the abbreviations attorneys use most. It is not exhaustive — for an unusual reporter, court, journal, or party word, follow the same patterns and consult a full abbreviation table. These abbreviations track the required-abbreviation tables in *The Indigo Book* (reporters and courts, T1 & T9; case-name words, T11; geographic terms, T12; document subdivisions, T13; explanatory phrases, T14; publishing terms, T16; months, T17; periodical words, T18). Those tables are facts required by courts, not anyone's property; when in doubt, consult the corresponding Indigo Book table.

## Spacing and periods — the rules that govern every abbreviation

- **Close up adjacent single capitals**, treating ordinal numbers and `&` as single capitals: U.S., S.D.N.Y., N.W.2d, D.C. Cir. (here "D.C." closes up, then a space before "Cir.").
- **Keep a space between a single capital and a longer (multi-letter) abbreviation:** D. Mass., S.D. Cal., D. Conn., F. Supp.
- **Every abbreviation ends in a period**, except where the last letters are joined by an apostrophe (Ass'n, Int'l, Dep't, Comm'n, Sec'y) and except the postal-free state forms used in some reporters.

## Reporters

Federal:

- U.S. (United States Reports) — U.S. Supreme Court, official.
- S. Ct. (Supreme Court Reporter); L. Ed., L. Ed. 2d (Lawyers' Edition) — unofficial Supreme Court.
- F., F.2d, F.3d (Federal Reporter) — U.S. courts of appeals.
- F. App'x (Federal Appendix) — unpublished court of appeals opinions.
- F. Supp., F. Supp. 2d, F. Supp. 3d (Federal Supplement) — U.S. district courts.
- F.R.D. (Federal Rules Decisions); B.R. (Bankruptcy Reporter); Fed. Cl. (Federal Claims); T.C. (Tax Court).

Regional (state courts):

- A., A.2d, A.3d (Atlantic).
- N.E., N.E.2d, N.E.3d (North Eastern).
- N.W., N.W.2d (North Western).
- P., P.2d, P.3d (Pacific).
- S.E., S.E.2d (South Eastern).
- So., So. 2d, So. 3d (Southern).
- S.W., S.W.2d, S.W.3d (South Western).

State-specific (examples): Cal. Rptr., Cal. Rptr. 2d, Cal. Rptr. 3d; N.Y.S., N.Y.S.2d, N.Y.S.3d; Tex. (Texas Reports, historical).

Publishers add new numbered series to a reporter over time (a first series, then 2d, 3d, and beyond). Cite whichever series the volume you are citing belongs to; the abbreviation pattern is the same.

## Courts (for the parenthetical)

- **U.S. Supreme Court:** no abbreviation — the year alone appears in the parenthetical.
- **U.S. courts of appeals:** 1st Cir., 2d Cir., 3d Cir., 4th Cir. … 11th Cir., D.C. Cir., Fed. Cir. (Note the ordinals: 2d and 3d, not "2nd"/"3rd.")
- **U.S. district courts:** the district letter(s) + the geographic abbreviation — D. Mass., D.D.C., S.D.N.Y., E.D. Pa., N.D. Cal., W.D. Tex., M.D. Fla., D. Idaho.
- **State courts:** the state's highest court is shown by the state abbreviation alone (Mich., Tex., Cal., N.Y.). Intermediate and trial courts add a court abbreviation — Tex. App.; Cal. Ct. App.; N.Y. App. Div.; Wash. Ct. App.; Ill. App. Ct.; N.Y. Sup. Ct.

## Common case-name word abbreviations (in citation clauses)

& (and) · Ass'n (Association) · Auth. (Authority) · Bd. (Board) · Bros. (Brothers) · Co. (Company) · Comm'n (Commission) · Comm. (Committee) · Coop. (Cooperative) · Corp. (Corporation) · Ctr. (Center) · Dep't (Department) · Dev. (Development) · Dist. (District) · Div. (Division) · Envtl. (Environmental) · Fed'n (Federation) · Found. (Foundation) · Hosp. (Hospital) · Indus. (Industries / Industrial) · Inc. (Incorporated) · Ins. (Insurance) · Int'l (International) · Lab. (Laboratory / Labor) · Ltd. (Limited) · Mfg. (Manufacturing) · Mgmt. (Management) · Mut. (Mutual) · Nat'l (National) · No. (Number) · Pharm. (Pharmaceutical / Pharmaceuticals) · Prod., Prods. (Product, Products) · R.R. (Railroad) · Ry. (Railway) · Sav. (Savings) · Sec. (Securities) · Sec'y (Secretary) · Servs. (Services) · Sys. (System, Systems) · Tech. (Technology, Technologies) · Tel. (Telephone / Telegraph) · Transp. (Transportation) · Univ. (University)

**Allowed even in a textual sentence:** only **&, Ass'n, Bros., Co., Corp., Inc., Ltd., and No.** Spell out all the others when the case name is part of your prose.

**Procedural phrases (italicized, part of the name):** *In re*, *Ex parte*, *... ex rel. ...*

## Geographic terms (case-name style)

These differ from postal codes. Common forms: Ala., Ariz., Ark., Cal., Colo., Conn., Del., Fla., Ga., Haw., Idaho, Ill., Ind., Iowa, Kan., Ky., La., Me., Md., Mass., Mich., Minn., Miss., Mo., Mont., Neb., Nev., N.H., N.J., N.M., N.Y., N.C., N.D., Ohio, Okla., Or., Pa., R.I., S.C., S.D., Tenn., Tex., Utah, Vt., Va., Wash., W. Va., Wis., Wyo. Use "U.S." only as an adjective; never abbreviate "United States" as a named party.

## History and explanatory phrases (italicized)

*aff'd*, *aff'g*, *rev'd*, *rev'g*, *vacated*, *remanded*, *modified*, *cert. denied*, *cert. granted*, *reh'g denied*, *reh'g en banc granted*, *overruled by*, *abrogated by*, *superseded by statute*, *sub nom.*, *en banc*.

## Months

Jan., Feb., Mar., Apr., May, June, July, Aug., Sept., Oct., Nov., Dec. (Note: "Sept." has four letters; May, June, and July are not abbreviated.)

## Numbers and symbols

- Spell out the numbers zero through ninety-nine; use numerals for 100 and above. (Some attorneys follow the looser journalism convention of spelling out one through nine and using numerals from 10 up — be consistent within a document, and follow local rules if they specify.)
- Use numerals regardless for section and rule numbers, dates, page and pincite numbers, percentages, and the like.
- Spell out a number that begins a sentence.
- Use `§` for "section" and `§§` for multiple sections; `¶` and `¶¶` for paragraphs. Put a non-breaking space between the symbol and the number. Spell out "Section" when it begins a sentence.
- Use a comma in numbers of five or more digits (52,782).

# Statutes, Constitutions, Regulations, and Other Government Materials

Nothing in a statute, constitution, or regulation citation is italicized. The section symbol is `§` (use `§§` for a span of sections). Put a non-breaking space between `§` and the number. (Real code citations are used below where they are simply facts; named acts, bills, and agency matters are illustrative.)

## Federal statutes

A full citation has three parts: the name of the act (when useful), the source where it is found, and a parenthetical with the year.

Pattern: `<Title> U.S.C. § <Section> (<Year>).`

- 1 U.S.C. § 1 (2018).
- A span of sections: 29 U.S.C. §§ 201–219 (2018).
- Subsections and clauses: 17 U.S.C. § 106(1) (2018); 28 U.S.C. § 1391(b)(2) (2018).
- With the act's popular name and original section number: Securities Exchange Act of 1934 § 10(b), 15 U.S.C. § 78j(b) (2018). (Here "§ 10(b)" is the section of the act; "§ 78j(b)" is where it sits in the Code.)
- Unofficial/annotated code — add the publisher to the parenthetical: 42 U.S.C.A. § 1983 (West 2020).

Cite the **official U.S. Code (U.S.C.)** when you can. The U.S.C. is recodified every six years (2000, 2006, 2012, 2018, …); cite the most recent edition that contains the version of the statute you mean, and add a cumulative-supplement parenthetical when the provision was amended after that edition: 47 U.S.C. § 223 (2018 & Supp. II 2020). If the U.S.C. is unavailable, cite an unofficial code — **U.S.C.A. is preferred** (West), then U.S.C.S. (LexisNexis) — naming the publisher in the parenthetical: 18 U.S.C.A. §§ 2701–2711 (West 2000); 30 U.S.C.S. §§ 181–287 (LexisNexis 2015). If neither code carries the provision, cite the session laws (below).

The year is the **edition of the code**, not the year the statute was enacted. *The Indigo Book* includes the edition year; some attorneys and courts omit it for the current official Code, but keep it when in doubt or when a court expects it.

### Session laws (statutes not yet codified, or cited as enacted)

Pattern: `<Name>, Pub. L. No. <Congress>-<Number>, § <section>, <Volume> Stat. <Page> (<Year>).`

- Endangered Habitats Protection Act, Pub. L. No. 118-25, § 4, 137 Stat. 412, 418 (2023).

## State statutes

Cite the official code where one exists; the format varies by state. A typical citation lists the abbreviated code name, the section number(s), and a parenthetical with the publisher (for an unofficial code) and the year of the edition.

- Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code Ann. § 16.003 (West 2017).
- Cal. Civ. Code § 1542 (West 2019).
- N.Y. Gen. Oblig. Law § 5-701 (McKinney 2020).
- Fla. Stat. § 95.11 (2022). (official code — no publisher in the parenthetical)

## Procedural rules, restatements, and uniform acts

- Federal rules — no date: Fed. R. Civ. P. 12(b)(6); Fed. R. Evid. 401; Fed. R. App. P. 4; Fed. R. Crim. P. 11. Local rules: e.g., S.D.N.Y. Civ. R. 56.1.
- Restatements: Restatement (Second) of Torts § 402A (Am. Law Inst. 1965). With a comment: Restatement (Second) of Torts § 402A cmt. i (Am. Law Inst. 1965).
- Uniform acts and model codes — credit the promulgating body. The Uniform Commercial Code is a joint product of two organizations: U.C.C. § 2-314 (Am. Law Inst. & Unif. Law Comm'n 2017); Model Penal Code § 2.02 (Am. Law Inst. 1985). (In everyday practice the publisher/date parenthetical for these is often dropped — U.C.C. § 2-314.)
- Sentencing Guidelines: U.S. Sentencing Guidelines Manual § 3E1.1 (U.S. Sentencing Comm'n 2021).

## Federal tax materials

You may replace "26 U.S.C." with "I.R.C." In a court document discussing only current tax law, you may omit the year and publisher parenthetical.

- 26 U.S.C. § 162 (2018) → I.R.C. § 162.
- Treasury regulations: Treas. Reg. § 1.162-1 (2019). Temporary regulations: Temp. Treas. Reg. § 1.274-5T(c)(6) (1985).
- Treasury determinations — Revenue Rulings, Revenue Procedures, and Treasury Decisions: Rev. Rul. 81-225, 1981-2 C.B. 12; Rev. Proc. 97-27, 1997-21 I.R.B. 11; T.D. 2135, 17 Treas. Dec. Int. Rev. 39 (1915). Cite to the Cumulative Bulletin (C.B.) first, then the Internal Revenue Bulletin (I.R.B.).

## Constitutions

Cite by the level of subdivision; no date for a constitution currently in force.

- U.S. Const. art. I, § 8, cl. 3.
- U.S. Const. amend. XIV, § 1.
- U.S. Const. pmbl.
- State: Tex. Const. art. I, § 9.

Cite a repealed or superseded provision with a parenthetical date (e.g., "(repealed 1933)").

## Regulations and other administrative materials

- **Code of Federal Regulations** — by title, section (or part), and year: 40 C.F.R. § 261.4 (2023); 29 C.F.R. § 1910.132 (2023).
- **Federal Register** — name of the rule, then volume, "Fed. Reg.," the page on which it begins, and the date; note where it will be codified if known. Page numbers take commas: Hazardous Waste Management System, 88 Fed. Reg. 12,345 (Mar. 1, 2023) (to be codified at 40 C.F.R. pt. 261).
- **Agency adjudications** — cite by the first-listed private party (or the official subject-matter title), then the reporter: Coastal Freight Co., 355 N.L.R.B. 112 (2010).
- **Executive orders** — Exec. Order No. 14,099, 88 Fed. Reg. 22,100 (Apr. 12, 2023).

## Legislative materials

A full citation generally includes the title or description, the abbreviated body, the number assigned, the number of the Congress and/or session, and the year.

- **Enacted federal bill** (cite by name when relevant): Small Business Regulatory Relief Act, S. 1421, 118th Cong. § 3 (2023).
- **Unenacted federal bill:** Consumer Data Privacy Act, H.R. 884, 118th Cong. § 2(a) (2023).
- **Congressional report:** H.R. Rep. No. 118-42, at 12 (2023); S. Rep. No. 117-130, at 7 (2022).
- **Congressional hearing** (title italicized): *Oversight of Federal Data Privacy Practices: Hearing Before the Subcomm. on Consumer Prot. & Com. of the H. Comm. on Energy & Commerce*, 118th Cong. 14 (2023).
- **Congressional Record:** 168 Cong. Rec. 14,205 (2022).
- **State bills and resolutions:** name the legislative body, the bill/resolution number, the number of the session, and a parenthetical with the state and year: H.B. 218, 2023 Leg., 88th Sess. (Tex. 2023).

## Short forms

For statutes and regulations, use *id.* for the immediately preceding authority. For a different provision in the same title, combine *id.* with the new section.

| Full citation | Same provision | Different provision, same title |
|---|---|---|
| 28 U.S.C. § 1391 (2018). | *Id.* | *Id.* § 1404. |
| 40 C.F.R. § 261.4 (2023). | *Id.* | *Id.* § 261.5(a)(1). |

Otherwise, a statute short form repeats the code and section: 42 U.S.C. § 1983.

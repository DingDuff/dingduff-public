# Clarity Edit

A disciplined revision pass over drafted prose — making it **concise, precise, simple, and clear** at the word, sentence, and paragraph level. Run once, after the substance is settled. This pass changes *how* the prose reads, never *what it says*.

## Preserve legal substance

- Don't change a legal **conclusion**, **holding**, the sense of a **rule**, or an outcome.
- Don't "simplify" a **term of art** into inaccuracy (*summary judgment*, *consideration*, *scienter*, *proximate cause* stay). Plain-language editing targets needless legalese (*hereinafter*, *said party*), not load-bearing terms.
- Don't alter **quoted material**.
- When precision and simplicity conflict, **precision wins**. If a clarity fix would change meaning, leave it and flag it.

## Four fundamentals

Edit toward **conciseness, precision, simplicity, clarity**; when they tension, resolve precision → clarity → conciseness → simplicity.

## Pass 1 — Sentences (the fault checklist)

| Fault | Fix | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Passive voice | Active, real-actor subject | *The motion was granted by the court* → *The court granted the motion.* |
| Nominalization | Restore the verb | *made an objection to* → *objected to*; *reached a decision* → *decided.* |
| Buried subject/verb | Subject up front; keep S-V-O together | move long intervening clauses out of the gap |
| Overlong sentence | One main idea per sentence; split | aim ~20 words average, varied |
| Throat-clearers / expletives | Cut to the substance | *It is important to note that the statute requires…* → *The statute requires…* |
| Excessive / compound prepositions | Recast; one word | *in order to* → *to*; *prior to* → *before*; *in the event that* → *if* |
| Faulty parallelism | Align grammatical form | *negligent, reckless, and acted with malice* → *negligent, reckless, and malicious* |
| Misplaced / dangling modifier | Put the modifier next to what it modifies | — |

## Pass 2 — Words

Cut legalese/archaisms (*hereinafter, aforesaid, said, such [as pronoun], pursuant to, prior to, notwithstanding the foregoing*) → plain equivalents. Kill redundant doublets (*each and every, null and void, cease and desist*) → one word. Keep terms of art. Choose the precise word (watch *which/that, may/shall/must, and/or, affect/effect*). Use defined terms **consistently** — elegant variation of a legal term implies a new meaning.

## Pass 3 — Paragraphs and flow

- **Topic sentence** opens each paragraph with its point (coordinate with `organization.md` if structure is in play).
- **Given-new flow** — open a sentence with familiar information, end with the new; thread each sentence's new idea into the next sentence's opening. This is the mechanics of "flow."
- **One point per paragraph;** split paragraphs that sprawl.
- **Stress position** — put the words you want remembered at the *end* of the sentence; keep citations out of that slot.

## Finish

Edit sentences → words → paragraph flow, then **read it aloud**. Anything that makes you stumble or reread is a defect. Confirm no edit changed a legal meaning, a term of art, or a quotation.

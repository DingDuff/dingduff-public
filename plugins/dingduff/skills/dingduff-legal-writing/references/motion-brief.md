# Motion Brief (Persuasive, Trial Court)

A persuasive trial-court brief or memorandum of points and authorities — for a motion or an opposition (motion to dismiss, summary judgment, in limine, compel, etc.). Draft it from the proof skeleton produced by `dingduff-legal-analysis` (persuasive ordering).

## Persuade within candor and the governing standard

- **Advocacy on a sound footing.** Persuasion (`persuasion.md`) amplifies a correct analysis; build the analysis first.
- **Candor.** Disclose adverse **controlling** authority; confront damaging facts; never misstate law or record.
- **Meet the governing standard.** Every motion is decided under a legal test (see the standard-of-review material in `components.md`). Frame the facts and argument to satisfy (or defeat) that test explicitly.
- **Defer to local rules** — page limits, formatting, meet-and-confer certifications, chambers rules.

## Anatomy (defer to local rules)

1. **Caption / title** — the court's caption and a title naming the motion.
2. **Introduction** — short and punchy: what you want, why you win, the theme. The most-read part; make it earn the ruling.
3. **Statement of Facts** — persuasive, accurate, record-cited. See `components.md`.
4. **Legal Standard** — the governing standard for this motion, with authority. See `components.md`.
5. **Argument** — organized by point headings (`components.md`); each point a conclusion-first proof (CRAC/CREAC) from the skeleton, applying the rule to the facts, confronting the opponent.
6. **Conclusion / Prayer for Relief** — state precisely the relief requested.
7. **Signature** and **certificate of service** (and any required certifications).

## Posture

- **Movant** must affirmatively establish entitlement under the standard.
- **Opponent** need only defeat one necessary element — lead with the strongest defeater.
- The standard drives everything: on summary judgment, fight over what's genuinely disputed and material; on a motion to dismiss, argue from the pleaded facts taken as true. Don't argue the wrong posture.

## Workflow

Analyze (`dingduff-legal-analysis`, persuasive) → draft the parts from the skeleton (facts + standard + point headings from `components.md`) → organize (`organization.md`) → persuasion pass (`persuasion.md`) → clarity edit (`clarity.md`) → cite-check & format.

## Self-check

Analysis sound and skeleton-anchored before persuasion; governing standard stated and the argument framed to meet/defeat it; adverse controlling authority disclosed; point headings argue the case in the TOC; introduction sells the result; relief precisely stated; local rules satisfied; citations checked.

## Going further (dispositive / complex motions)

Follow the "Going further" guidance in `dingduff-legal-analysis`. Build the **opponent's brief** before yours (their best issues, framings, authorities, facts) and pre-empt each. Present each dispositive argument with **fallbacks** (if the court rejects ground A, ground B still wins), argued in the alternative. For evidence-based motions, work the record exhaustively — every material fact tied to admissible evidence with a pincite; genuine disputes isolated from immaterial ones.
